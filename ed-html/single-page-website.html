Mish Mash

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 2 green bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 3 tomatoes, peeled and seeded
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2.25 cups sirene or feta cheese , crumbled
  • 6 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Directions

  1. Finely dice bell peppers. Coarsely chop tomatoes.
  2. In a large heavy skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat.
    First, add the onions and garlic, and sauté for about 2 minutes, until the onions are soft, but not completely cooked.
  3. Add the bell peppers and mix well. Cover and cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the tomatoes, stir, cover and cook for another 5 to 7 minutes.
  5. Add the sirene or feta and mix. Pour the eggs. Stir occasionally until the mixture is less liquid.
  6. Finally add the parsley and cook for a minute before removing from heat.
  7. Serve hot and Enjoy!


Banitsa

Ingredients

  • 12 sheets phyllo dough
  • 8 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup sirene (Bulgarian cheese) or feta, coarsely crumbled
  • 1 1/2 cups Bulgarian yogurt (preferably sheep's milk yogurt)
  • 4 whole eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Directions

  1. Whisk yogurt with 4 whole eggs.
  2. Add cheese, salt and pepper and mix.
  3. Melt the butter.
  4. Take two sheets of phyllo and brush the top sheet with melted butter.
  5. Superimpose a third sheet on the first two and brush the top with butter.
  6. Turn the three sheets over and then brush the non coated side.
  7. Divide ¼ of the yogurt, egg and cheese mixture on top of the filo sheets.
  8. Then roll the whole preparation like a long sausage.
    Roll lengthwise to get the roll as long as possible.
  9. Repeat this process 3 more times to get 4 long rolls of three superimposed sheets of filo each.
  10. Place the springform pan on a baking sheet (or just circle on baking sheet covered with parchment paper).
  11. Begin by placing the first roll by sticking to the edge of the circle, then add the following forming a snail to the center.
  12. Beat egg yolk with the remaining melted butter and brush the whole banitsa.
  13. Bake at 400F/200C for about 25 minutes.
  14. The banitsa should be golden.
  15. It can be eaten either hot, warm or cold.


Tarator

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 cups Bulgarian yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and deseeded
  • 3/4 cup walnuts , chopped and lightly toasted
  • 3 tablespoons sunflower oil
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 small bunch dill , finely chopped
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Directions

  1. Beat the yogurt until it is smooth.
  2. In a blender, add the yoghurt, oil, garlic, cucumber, half of the walnuts, half of the dill.
  3. Blend until reaching a smooth consistency. Add salt and pepper.
  4. Serve chilled or top with ice cubes and sprinkle the remaining walnuts and dill.


Bean Soup

Ingredients

  • 1¼ cup dried white beans
  • 4 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion , finely chopped
  • 1 carrot , finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper , finely chopped
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1½ tablespoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoons dried spearmint
  • 2 tomatoes , finely chopped
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley , chopped

Directions

  1. Rinse the beans in cold running water. Place them in a large pot and soak them in cold water overnight.
  2. The following morning, discard the soaking water. Add 3 cups (750ml) of water to the beans in the pot. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  3. After 5 minutes, discard the water once again, leaving only the beans in the pan.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to the beans and fry over medium heat for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Add 4 cups of water to the beans. Then lower the heat and simmer the beans until they start becoming tender, about 45 minutes.
  6. In the meantime, add 2 tablespoons of oil to another pan. Then add the carrots and onion. Sauté for 8 minutes.
  7. Add the bell pepper, tomatoes, paprika, spearmint, salt and pepper and stir well. Continue cooking for 5 minutes.
  8. Add the mixture to the beans and water in the other pot. Keep the soup simmering until the beans are completely cooked but remain whole, about 20 minutes. Do not overcook them.
  9. Serve hot with parsley or grated cheese on top.


Ajvar

Ingredients

  • 9 cups red peppers
  • 2.25 cups eggplant
  • 1¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 teaspoons salt

Directions

  1. Poke a few holes in the eggplants, and roast them on a grill (ideally) or in the oven until soft and charred, about 30 to 40 minutes.
  2. Repeat the same operation with the peppers.
  3. Place the eggplants and the peppers in separate bowls, covered with plastic wrap. Let cool for at least 1 hour.
  4. Remove all the charred peel and seeds from the peppers and wash them well.
  5. Remove the peel of the eggplants with a knife.
  6. Place the eggplant, pepper, garlic cloves into a food processor (or a food mill) and pulse a few times until they are finely chopped.
  7. Heat the oil in a large pot. Add the mixture and stir with a wooden spoon. Add salt and vinegar.
  8. Cook over low to medium heat for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally so the ajvar doesn’t stick to the bottom of the pan.
  9. The end result can be canned, or stored in the fridge and consumed within a week.


Baklava

Ingredients

  • 5 cups sugar
  • 1 cup light olive oil, or as needed
  • 454 grams box filo/phyllo pastry, 36 cm by 46 cm (14 by 18 inch) sheets, cut in half crosswise)
  • 2 cups finely ground walnuts
  • Cinnamon in a shaker
  • 36 cm by 46 cm ceramic or glass baking dish

Directions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine sugar with 946 ml (4 cups) of water. Place over low heat and simmer until the sugar is melted. Allow to cool and refrigerate until well chilled, about 4 hours.
  2. Preheat oven to 162° C/325° F. Place the olive oil in a small saucepan over low heat; it will be used after the baklava is assembled and should be hot. Place two pastry sheets on the bottom of the casserole. Lightly sprinkle walnuts and a little cinnamon all over. Top with one sheet of pastry and again sprinkle with walnuts and cinnamon. Repeat until all the sheets are used, saving two unblemished sheets for placing on top.
  3. Cut the baklava into diamond shapes, and immediately brush all the cut edges with hot oil. Bake until pastry is lightly browned and fragrant, about 1 hour. Remove from oven and immediately (and carefully) pour the cold sugar syrup over it. The entire baklava, including the top, should be saturated. (If necessary, adjust the position of the top diamonds.)
  4. Cover lightly with waxed paper, and allow to soak for 1-2 hours. Drain and discard the sugar syrup. Cover and store at room temperature
